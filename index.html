Research Focus

My primary research focus is the development of statistical models to assess population-level trends in cause-specific and all-cause mortality, on both national and global levels. A majority of my work has been on estimation and projection of maternal mortality rates, specifically developing a statistical approach to assess misreporting errors in national level mortality data. The methodological challenges in this area of research comes from very sparse and limited data on true or gold-standard mortality rates, which are used as inputs to assess national and global levels of data quality parameters. Additionally, we extend our aims to model data quality parameters across different populations and across time. To address these aims, I have developed a Bayesian approach to modeling measurement error with the use of Bayesian hierarchical methods, and a sequential approach to extend to different populations without gold-standard data. I have collaborated with the World Health Organization and Johns Hopkins University.
